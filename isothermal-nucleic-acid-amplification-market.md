<p><p>Isothermal Nucleic Acid Amplification techniques enable DNA or RNA amplification at a constant temperature, eliminating the need for thermal cycling. Key market types include LAMP (Loop-mediated Isothermal Amplification), which is quick and highly specific, making it ideal for point-of-care diagnostics. HDA (Helicase-dependent Amplification) utilizes helicase enzymes to separate strands, enhancing efficiency and yield. Other methods encompass various innovative isothermal techniques like RPA (Recombinase Polymerase Amplification) and ICA (Isothermal Circular Amplification), catering to diverse diagnostic and research needs in molecular biology.</p></p>

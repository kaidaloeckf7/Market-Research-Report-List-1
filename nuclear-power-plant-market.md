<p><p>The nuclear power plant market is categorized into several reactor types. Pressurized Water Reactors (PWR) use water as both coolant and moderator but keep it under pressure to prevent boiling. Boiling Water Reactors (BWR) allow water to boil directly in the reactor core, producing steam for power generation. Heavy Water Reactors (HWR) utilize heavy water as a moderator, offering increased fuel efficiency. Fast Reactors (FR) employ fast neutrons for fission, allowing the use of depleted uranium and reducing nuclear waste. Each type has distinct operational and economic characteristics.</p></p>

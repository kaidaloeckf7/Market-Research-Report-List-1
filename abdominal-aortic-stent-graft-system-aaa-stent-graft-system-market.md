The unibody stent graft is a single-piece design, ideal for straightforward cases, offering simplicity in deployment and stability. In contrast, the bifurcated stent graft consists of two branches, specifically designed to accommodate varying anatomical configurations and provide better blood flow to the iliac arteries. Both types are essential for effectively managing different patient needs and anatomical challenges.</p></p>
